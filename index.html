Executive Directors

Executive directors hold an operational position in the company. They are responsible for the day-to-day operations and results; best placed to develop, set, and implement strategy.

Non-executive Directors

Non-executive directors (NEDs) do not hold an executive position and may or may not be independent. NEDs contribute an outside perspective and greater impartiality in their judgments; additional external experience and knowledge; useful contacts.

Independent Directors

An independent director (ID) has no material relationship with the company beyond his or her directorship. IDs ensure the board includes individuals who exercise objective judgement for the exclusive benefit of the company and its shareholders.

An independent director (ID) is a director who has no material relationship with the company beyond his or her directorship. IDs ensure that the board includes individuals who can effectively exercise objective judgement for the exclusive benefit of the company and its shareholders.

Director Pay: The data provided by Spencer Stuart indicate that the average retainer for directors in the US is $91,000, but this varies widely depending on the size and type of corporation. To better align the interests of directors with those of shareholders, firms have increasingly paid directors in stock rather than cash, and many require that directors invest their own money in the firm’s shares. Many companies pay directors a bonus for each board meeting attended, and this even includes teleconferences—a way that companies are handling the geographic divide and demands of a global workplace.
